Tips To Avoid Bullying







Tips Avoid Bullying

  • Appear Confident : Do not display insecurity, fear, and low self-esteem.
  • Make eye contact : Eye contact communicates self-confidence and self-esteem. So maintain a firm eye contact.
  • Dont consider you as a victim : Feeling like a victim makes you act like a victim. So feel bold and confident.
  • Be assertive : Assertive people stand up for their rights and are comfortable defending themselves or others against unfairness.
  • Build self-esteem : Esy self-esteem brings about more confidence and the capability to easily cope up with the bullies.
  • Nuture Friendships : Friends can help and save a kid from being bullied or to come out of past trauma of bullying
  • Be aware of bullying spots : If you are not ready to face a bully avoid going to places with very little adult supervision.
  • Never blame yourself : Never blame you for being bullied instead build a better version of yourself.

First line of defense against bullying is being prepared....

Appear More Confident
So what can you do? First, to ensure that others see you as a confident person and not an insecure person who can be controlled or victimised, make sure you work on developing your own self-esteem so that you truly are confident.
Make Eye Contact
Maintain eye contact with them and stand with good posture to boost your confidence while talking to them. After a few interactions, they won’t want to bully you since you aren’t reacting to them.
Dont be a victim
“To stop being a victim, you have to stop thinking like a victim .” Sometimes bad things happen to us that are completely out of our control.
Be Assertive
Body language is a universal language. You can communicate a lot of unspoken words with your body. When speaking, stand upright with your head held high and maintain eye contact with the person you are talking to in order to communicate confidence and self-assuredness.
Build your Self-Esteem
As your self-esteem increases, your confidence and sense of well-being are likely to soar. In addition to these suggestions, remember that you're worth special care. Be sure to: Take care of yourself.
Nuture Quality Friendships
Nurturing a healthy friendship, needs give and take on both sides. “If it feels too one-sided,then consider introducing boundaries to protect yourself and your energy”
No Bully Zones
Part of learning to be inclusive and being the change to maintain a no bully zone includes noticing when others are being bullied and speaking up to stop it.
Stop Blaming Yourself
Excessive self-blame can lead to negative emotional states. Balancing self-responsibility and self-compassion is vital for well-being. Techniques like reframing thoughts and practicing mindfulness can mitigate self-blame.
